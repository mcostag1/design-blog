Project 3 Inspo

Lack of seating in sci commons + cornell

Both Ali and I spend a fair amount of time in sci commons and cornell, and we've both been fced with the issue of a lack of seating. This is especially true during lunch rush hour, or late night in cornell.

Communication (taking online things, offline)

Swarthmore students tend to discuss topics (more specifically controversial topics) online and avoid having the conversations in person. We thought it would be interestesting to have a sort of physical data visualization/polling system in a highly populated area on campus (sci commons or shane). We first thought this would be interesting to have as a way for the students to communicate to the administration.

Avoiding Sharples crowds

Sharples doesn't have the capacity to fit the student body - and the lack of space and crowds get worse during rush hours. One way to remedy this issue would be to have some sort of system to let students know when sharples is crowded (which is heavily influenced by the time of day and what is on the menu that day). We were thinking of an abstract visualization based off of a live stream of sharples main dining room - a light display of some sort that would shine brighter or a different color when sharples was crowded, and dimmer when it was on the empty side.

AP Dorm

Our next two models were inspired by the AP dormitory. We wanted to emulate the geometric shape of the building as well as the windows. We used legos for our first model, since it was easy to maintain the geometric shape with lego pieces. The aim for our last model was to play with the symmetry and motion. We hapazardly stuck a piece of balsa wood in the middle to move pieces around, but ideally it would be a piece of wire or rod. Each rectangular frame is made from the cut out of a larger one, so all the frames should (theoretically) fit perfectly within each other. This would work out better with a laser cutter, but we think the general concept was achieved.
